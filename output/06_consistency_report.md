#### 1. WORLD RULE COMPLIANCE
- **ISSUE**: The presence of a digital trap laid by Duke in the café seems inconsistent with the established technology rules. While hacking is mentioned, the sudden alarm system appears overly advanced for a café setting, which may not have the same level of corporate security as the Neon Tower.
- **LOCATION**: Scene 8, "The Streets of San Hastina"
- **SEVERITY**: Major
- **SUGGESTED FIX**: Adjust the scene to indicate that the café is a front for a Kuru Corporation operation, thus justifying the advanced security measures.

---

#### 2. CHARACTER CONSISTENCY
- **ISSUE**: Brock's hesitation in the Blood Pit seems inconsistent with his established character as "The Beast," who is known for his brute strength and fierce loyalty. His sudden moral dilemma feels abrupt and lacks sufficient buildup.
- **LOCATION**: Scene 1, "The Blood Pit"
- **SEVERITY**: Major
- **SUGGESTED FIX**: Introduce earlier scenes or dialogue that hint at Brock's internal conflict regarding violence and loyalty, perhaps through conversations with Yash or Dahlia.

- **ISSUE**: Kaden's character arc is somewhat muddled. His loyalty to Duke is established, but his sudden decision to join the Pandavas feels rushed and lacks the necessary emotional buildup.
- **LOCATION**: Scene 11, "The Clash Ignites"
- **SEVERITY**: Major
- **SUGGESTED FIX**: Include more internal dialogue or flashbacks that illustrate Kaden's struggle with his loyalty to Duke versus his desire for acceptance from the Pandavas, leading to a more gradual realization.

---

#### 3. PLOT LOGIC
- **ISSUE**: The transition from the café scene to the subsequent scenes lacks clarity. The characters go from being compromised in the café to planning their next moves without addressing the immediate consequences of being marked by Duke.
- **LOCATION**: Scene 8, "The Streets of San Hastina" to Scene 9, "The Neon Tower"
- **SEVERITY**: Major
- **SUGGESTED FIX**: Include a scene that shows the immediate fallout from the café incident, such as Duke's reaction or the characters' need to lay low, to create a clearer cause-and-effect relationship.

---

#### 4. THEMATIC FIDELITY
- **ISSUE**: The theme of loyalty versus ambition is present but could be more deeply explored, particularly in Kaden's arc. His decision to join the Pandavas should reflect a more profound internal struggle that resonates with the overarching themes of the story.
- **LOCATION**: Throughout the story, particularly in Kaden's interactions.
- **SEVERITY**: Major
- **SUGGESTED FIX**: Expand on Kaden's backstory and his relationship with Duke, illustrating how his loyalty has been tested and how he ultimately chooses a different path.

---

#### 5. TONAL CONSISTENCY
- **ISSUE**: The tone shifts slightly between the intense fight scenes and the more philosophical discussions about justice and loyalty. While both elements are present, the transitions can feel jarring.
- **LOCATION**: Various scenes, particularly between the Blood Pit and the Oracle Lounge.
- **SEVERITY**: Minor
- **SUGGESTED FIX**: Smooth the transitions by incorporating more reflective moments during the fight scenes or adding action-oriented dialogue during the philosophical discussions.

---

#### 6. CULTURAL SENSITIVITY
- **ISSUE**: The portrayal of women in the Oracle Lounge scene could be seen as reinforcing stereotypes of women needing to rally around a male figure (Yash) for support, rather than showcasing their agency.
- **LOCATION**: Scene 2, "The Oracle Lounge"
- **SEVERITY**: Major
- **SUGGESTED FIX**: Empower Dahlia further by allowing her to take the lead in strategizing without relying on Yash's approval, showcasing her as an independent force.
